# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Atelie Guadalupe serves shoppers with very little tech literacy who need a clear, calm path from understanding the store to buying natural tallow-based products. They may arrive with practical questions about skin care, product ingredients, price, delivery, and whether the brand is trustworthy.

The primary job is to understand what the store sells, trust that the products are handmade and healthy, then buy or request guided help through WhatsApp when a product needs consultation.

## Product Purpose

Atelie Guadalupe is a public storefront for natural tallow products and artisanal goods, supported by an administrative area for managing products, orders, billing, users, and testimonials.

Success means the public experience makes the purpose of the store obvious, presents products with enough context for low-tech shoppers, and moves people confidently toward purchase. When a decision needs personal guidance, the interface should make WhatsApp consultation feel direct and expected.

## Brand Personality

Devout, artisanal, and healthy. The voice should feel rooted in faith, real craft, and care for the body without becoming abstract or overly polished.

The brand should feel like a store with a founder, a history, and products made by real hands. It should speak plainly, with warmth and conviction, so customers can understand what they are buying and why it matters.

## Anti-references

This should not look or feel like generic ecommerce, a luxury boutique, a dropshipping storefront, or a soulless corporate shop with no story and nothing to care about.

Avoid anonymous marketplace patterns, over-polished wellness-spa aesthetics, vague lifestyle copy, and interfaces that hide the product purpose behind decoration.

## Design Principles

1. Make the product purpose unmistakable. Visitors should quickly understand that the store sells natural tallow products and artisanal goods.
2. Lead with trust before polish. Founder story, real product imagery, plain ingredients, testimonials, and clear buying steps matter more than decorative flourish.
3. Design for low tech literacy. Labels, buttons, filters, prices, and consultation paths should be obvious without requiring ecommerce familiarity.
4. Keep faith and craft concrete. Religious and artisanal language should connect to real product care, making, ingredients, and service.
5. Treat admin as a tool. Public surfaces use the brand register; administrative screens use product-register clarity, density, and consistency.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Prioritize readable contrast, large tap targets, straightforward labels, predictable navigation, visible focus states, and reduced-motion support.

Because the primary audience includes people with little tech literacy, avoid hidden interactions, ambiguous icons without labels, and flows that depend on users knowing ecommerce conventions.
